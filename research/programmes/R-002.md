# R-002 execution protocol

## Test the claimed cross-lifecycle integration gap

Status: approved protocol; search strategy and construct map founder-approved
and merged. Stage A execution is authorised only when the mechanical authority
transition in PR #19 is merged. Stage B remains unauthorised.

This protocol defines the R-002 question, scope, eligibility, decision space,
roles, lifecycle and governance boundaries. It contains no R-002 evidence or
findings and assumes no conclusion.

## Objective

Answer one question:

> Do existing organisational-memory, knowledge-management, traceability,
> provenance and architecture practices already cover what OME claims is a
> cross-lifecycle integration gap?

R-002 must evaluate existing practice on its strongest documented terms. Its
purpose is not to prove that OME is necessary, distinct or novel.

## Decision to enable

Retain, narrow, revise, reject, supersede or defer C-003:

> Existing organisational-memory, knowledge-management, traceability,
> decision-rationale and provenance practices leave a material cross-lifecycle
> integration gap.

The eventual decision must distinguish at least:

- absence of conceptual coverage;
- partial or fragmented coverage;
- complete coverage by one established approach;
- complete coverage through a deliberate composition of established practices;
- specified coverage that is not implemented or maintained;
- adoption, incentives, authority, ownership or interoperability failure; and
- absence of adequate evidence about whether a gap exists.

R-002 may also produce evidence relevant to C-005, which currently describes
OME as an engineering synthesis and research programme. C-005 does not inherit a
decision from C-003. Any material C-005 change requires a separate construct
assessment, independent review and mechanical state transition.

## Primary research question

Does an established approach, framework, standard, deliberate composition or
implemented practice ecosystem already connect:

> objective -> decision -> implementation -> operation -> learning

as a continuous, recoverable and maintained organisational-memory lifecycle?

This sequence is a neutral protocol boundary, not proposed OME terminology,
architecture or canon. PR #18 will define and challenge the construct map before
it is used for evidence assessment.

## Research questions

1. Which existing approaches claim cross-lifecycle integration rather than
   coverage of one stage, artifact or discipline?
2. Can compositions of established practices provide equivalent integration
   even if no single named framework does?
3. Do approaches connect objectives, evidence, decisions and rationale,
   implementation, operational outcomes and learning in practice?
4. Are relationships maintained through change and recoverable when needed?
5. What independently inspectable evidence supports claimed coverage, use,
   outcomes and burden?
6. Are observed discontinuities caused by missing practice or by adoption,
   incentives, authority, ownership, interoperability, data quality or cost?
7. When is integration unnecessary, harmful, too burdensome or inferior to
   bounded local practices?
8. What evidence would require C-003 or C-005 to be narrowed, revised, rejected,
   superseded or deferred?

## Scope

### In scope

- recognised engineering, organisational and knowledge practices that claim to
  connect more than one lifecycle stage;
- standards, frameworks, methods and reference models;
- deliberate, repeatable compositions of established practices;
- implemented practice ecosystems with inspectable design or outcome evidence;
- semantic, procedural, governance and interoperability mechanisms that
  maintain cross-lifecycle relationships;
- evidence about adoption, maintenance, burden, failure modes and outcomes; and
- counterexamples in which existing practice already provides sufficient
  coverage.

An approach may be federated. It need not use one repository, tool, vocabulary
or authority if its composition is explicit, recoverable and maintained.

### Out of scope

- product architecture, APIs, screens or implementation backlog;
- ContextBridge or any private commercial design;
- R-001, C-002 or their reopening conditions;
- invention or admission of OME terminology;
- canon, marketing or branding decisions;
- implementation, pilots, interviews or private organisational data;
- Stage B design or execution;
- tool feature inventories without an evaluable practice; and
- novelty claims inferred from failure to find a source.

## Unit of analysis

The unit may be:

- a single established approach;
- a documented composition of approaches;
- a standard or reference framework;
- an implemented organisational practice; or
- a tool ecosystem only when the practice and integration can be evaluated
  independently of vendor positioning.

An isolated artifact, hyperlink, dashboard or repository is not by itself
cross-lifecycle integration. Conversely, no branded all-in-one framework is
required if a repeatable composition provides equivalent coverage.

## Inclusion criteria

Future Stage A records may include:

- authoritative specifications or standards defining relevant lifecycle
  coverage or integration;
- primary empirical research on implementation, maintenance, outcomes or
  burden;
- systematic, scoping or authoritative critical reviews;
- documented compositions with explicit relationships and governance;
- credible implementation studies, including negative and null outcomes; and
- strong disconfirming cases in which established practice already solves the
  alleged problem.

Inclusion in Stage A will not imply evidential support for C-003. Direction must
be assigned against the precise claim after source appraisal.

## Exclusion from direct support

The following may remain contextual but cannot directly support C-003:

- vendor or marketing claims without independently inspectable evidence;
- diagrams that imply integration but define no maintained relationships;
- isolated trace links or repositories presented as full-lifecycle coverage;
- failures that do not distinguish missing practice from poor adoption,
  incentives, configuration, governance or implementation;
- internal OME synthesis used as external proof;
- private product knowledge or commercial analysis; and
- absence of a discovered approach treated as evidence of nonexistence.

Exclusion reasons must be recorded. Inconvenient or duplicative approaches must
not be erased.

## Competing explanations to steelman

- A mature established approach already supplies the claimed lifecycle.
- A deliberate composition is sufficient and preferable to a unified method.
- Conceptual coverage exists, but organisations fail to adopt or maintain it.
- Interoperability, governance or data quality explains discontinuity better
  than a missing engineering practice.
- Integration burden, surveillance, rigidity, privacy risk or maintenance cost
  exceeds its benefit.
- Different lifecycle objects change at incompatible rates, making continuous
  integration impractical or undesirable.
- Existing approaches cover the lifecycle aspirationally but lack evidence of
  maintained use or outcomes.
- Any gap is restricted to a narrow context and cannot support a general claim.

## Success criteria

R-002 succeeds if reviewed evidence supports any one of these conclusions:

1. Existing practice already covers the gap.
2. Existing practice partially covers the gap.
3. Existing practice covers adjacent problems but not the integrated gap.
4. There is no adequate evidence that such a gap exists.

All four are valid outcomes. None is preferred in advance. The fourth outcome
is epistemic uncertainty, not proof that the gap is absent.

The later claim assessment must translate the evidence into the repository's
formal dispositions rather than treat these success states as new lifecycle
values.

## What would convince us we are wrong?

- An established approach or repeatable composition already provides adequate
  cross-lifecycle coverage.
- The apparent gap disappears when approaches are evaluated at their intended
  scope or in combination.
- Discontinuity is explained more adequately by adoption, incentives,
  governance, authority, interoperability or cost.
- Integration introduces net harm or burden in the relevant contexts.
- The construct cannot be defined without imposing an OME-specific model.
- Available evidence cannot distinguish specified capability from maintained
  practice, requiring deferral.

These are successful research outcomes, not programme failures.

## Roles and accountability

Before execution begins, assign:

- **Research Lead:** owns protocol execution, source selection, synthesis and
  protocol-change records;
- **Evidence Reviewer:** independently reviews provenance, inclusion,
  appraisal, direction and missing-evidence risk;
- **Architecture Reviewer:** challenges the construct boundary and detects
  renamed or duplicated established practice; and
- **Accountable Approver:** approves material protocols, deviations, gates and
  claim decisions.

Responsibilities and minimum separation follow
[`research/ROLES.md`](../ROLES.md). The Research Lead cannot solely approve an
approach's coverage classification or the C-003 decision. Any role overlap must
remain explicit.

## Protocol-change rule

Changes follow the repository's
[research conduct guidelines](../RESEARCH_GUIDELINES.md). Each amendment must
record:

1. the prior protocol;
2. what changed and when;
3. why it changed;
4. whether observed material could have influenced the change; and
5. the expected effect on bias, scope and interpretation.

Do not silently revise the objective, eligibility or success criteria after an
approach is found.

## R-001 freeze boundary

R-001 is frozen at its approved stopping condition. C-002 remains `Assessed`
with disposition `Defer`, confidence `Very low` and classification `Hypothesis`.

R-002 must not:

- reopen, redesign or improve C-002;
- use C-002 as an established premise;
- treat R-002 evidence as a reopening trigger without a separate reviewed
  proposal applying the recorded conditions; or
- design or begin R-001 Stage B.

The authoritative C-002 baseline is recorded at merge commit `11b0088` and in
the current Claim Register.

## Public and private boundary

The public repository may contain the protocol, public search provenance,
authoritative public sources, comparison methods, evidence appraisals and claim
decisions.

It must not contain ContextBridge architecture, product differentiation,
implementation plans, customer or pilot information, security design, pricing,
commercial strategy, private observations or private product backlog.

Commercial tools may be evaluated using public evidence. Private product
knowledge cannot be used as public proof.

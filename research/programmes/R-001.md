# R-001 research programme

## Characterise consequential organisational reconstruction

Status: approved protocol; investigation not started.

This document designs the investigation. It contains no R-001 findings and does
not change the classification, confidence or disposition of C-002.

## Decision to enable

Retain, narrow, revise, reject or defer C-002:

> Organisations incur consequential reconstruction work because relevant
> reasoning cannot be retrieved when needed.

The decision must identify the contexts, mechanisms and outcomes for which the
claim is or is not justified.

## Research questions

1. What established constructs describe reconstruction of previously available
   organisational reasoning?
2. Under what conditions does inability to retrieve or apply that reasoning
   lead to observable reconstruction work?
3. What consequences are reported, and how directly are they measured?
4. Which alternative explanations account for the same observations?
5. When is forgetting, rediscovery or reconstruction neutral or beneficial?
6. Which boundary conditions prevent responsible generalisation?

## Working construct

For selection purposes, **organisational reconstruction** means effort by one or
more organisational actors to re-establish reasoning, context or knowledge that
was previously available to the organisation but cannot be retrieved or applied
when needed.

The working construct is a search aid, not proposed OME terminology. It excludes:

- discovery of something the organisation has not previously known;
- planned re-evaluation prompted by materially changed conditions;
- deliberate redundancy or independent verification where reconstruction is the
  intended control;
- inability to use knowledge because access is correctly prohibited; and
- ordinary execution work incorrectly relabelled as “reconstruction.”

The investigation must revise or abandon this construct if established terms
provide a better account.

## Competing explanations to steelman

- The information never existed in a usable organisational form.
- The information exists and is retrievable, but incentives, authority, time or
  coordination prevent its use.
- Staff turnover, workload, information overload or changing context explains
  the effect better than organisational forgetting.
- Reconstruction is beneficial because it detects obsolete assumptions,
  creates independence or adapts knowledge to a new context.
- Existing knowledge-management, decision-rationale or traceability practices
  address the problem when implemented competently; observed failures are
  adoption failures.
- Reported costs are ordinary discovery, rework or technical debt without a
  distinct memory mechanism.

### Stage A — Construct and vocabulary map

Search authoritative reviews and primary research across organisational memory
and forgetting, organisational learning and unlearning, knowledge transfer,
transactive memory, expertise loss, decision-rationale loss, requirements
communication and rediscovery/rework.

Outputs:

- established constructs and synonyms;
- candidate mechanisms and boundary conditions;
- strongest competing explanations;
- refined eligibility criteria and search strings; and
- evidence records for sources that bear directly on C-002.

Gate: the Research Lead and Evidence Reviewer agree that the construct is
specific enough to search without assuming the OME framing. Otherwise revise
the protocol before Stage B and record the deviation.

### Stage B — Focused evidence review

Freeze and timestamp the Stage B protocol before running the final searches.
Record databases, interfaces, full search strings, dates, filters, language
constraints, deduplication and citation-search procedures.

Use multiple relevant scholarly indexes where access permits. Supplement with
backward and forward citation searching and authoritative grey literature.
Searches must include disconfirming concepts such as beneficial forgetting,
unlearning, productive rediscovery, documentation/traceability burden and null
or negligible effects.

Screening and exclusion decisions are logged. The Evidence Reviewer audits all
borderline exclusions and a sample of other exclusions. Disagreements and their
resolution remain visible.

### Stage C — Empirical-research gate

Do not default to interviews or a pilot. After Stage B, decide whether the
literature can support a claim disposition.

Propose field research only if a material uncertainty remains and an ethical,
feasible design could discriminate between C-002 and its alternatives. Any
interview, observation or organisational-data study requires a separate reviewed
protocol covering consent, privacy, sampling, analysis, disclosure and data
retention.

## Eligibility framework

### Include

- empirical studies that observe inability to retrieve or apply previously
  available organisational reasoning, or a close established construct;
- empirical studies that measure related reconstruction effort or consequences;
- primary qualitative research capable of identifying mechanisms or boundary
  conditions;
- systematic or authoritative reviews used to locate and contextualise primary
  studies; and
- counterevidence concerning beneficial forgetting, productive rediscovery or
  effective existing practices.

### Exclude from direct support

- conceptual argument without empirical observation;
- vendor claims, unsourced commentary and marketing case studies;
- studies where prior organisational availability cannot be distinguished from
  new discovery;
- evidence about individual recall with no defensible organisational mechanism;
  and
- sources whose relevant result cannot be separated from an incompatible
  population, context or outcome.

Excluded sources may remain contextual evidence. Record an exclusion reason;
do not erase inconvenient sources.

## Outcomes and extraction

Extract separately where available:

- reconstruction time and effort;
- delay, rework or duplicated work;
- error, incident or decision-quality effects;
- loss or recovery of rationale and explanatory context;
- learning or adaptation effects;
- beneficial consequences of forgetting or independent rediscovery;
- population, setting, unit of analysis and time horizon;
- proposed causal mechanism and alternative explanations; and
- intervention, comparison and implementation conditions.

Use the Evidence Register fields and lifecycle. Separate a study's reported
finding from the programme's claim-specific interpretation.

## Bias controls

- Preserve the protocol and all deviations in Git before synthesis conclusions.
- Search for disconfirming evidence during construct mapping, not after a
  favourable narrative forms.
- Do not exclude based on direction or statistical significance.
- Assess shared datasets, repeated intellectual origins and non-reporting risk.
- Appraise directness, method and relevance separately from source prestige.
- Have the Evidence Reviewer challenge the strongest supporting and conflicting
  evidence.
- Report uncertainty, missing evidence and plausible alternative explanations.
- Disclose OME's intellectual and prospective commercial interest in the result.

## Search stopping rule

The final protocol records a fixed search date. Query expansion may stop when
two documented consecutive expansion rounds add no new eligible construct,
mechanism or primary-study family, and backward/forward searching of the central
sources is complete. Resource limits and inaccessible databases are reported as
limitations, not reframed as saturation.

## Learning and decision rules

No source count determines the outcome. Apply the repository's
[confidence and decision rules](../operating-system/DECISION_RULES.md).

- **Retain or narrow:** direct, relevant evidence supports a bounded version of
  C-002 after counterevidence and alternatives are addressed.
- **Revise:** the phenomenon is supported but an established construct,
  different mechanism or different outcome better represents it.
- **Reject:** credible evidence or analysis shows the proposed relationship is
  absent, immaterial or explained more adequately without C-002.
- **Defer:** evidence is too indirect, sparse, biased or inconsistent to
  discriminate responsibly.

A useful result may be that C-002 is false, too broad or not distinct. That
still completes the research decision.

## Reporting foundations

The review should use PRISMA 2020 reporting elements where applicable and state
where this focused, interdisciplinary review departs from them. Bias and missing
evidence should be considered using relevant principles from the Cochrane
Handbook, without implying that organisational research is equivalent to a
clinical intervention review.

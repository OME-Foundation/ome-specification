# R-001 Stage A Gate Review

Status: approved; construct assessment permitted while Stage A remains
incomplete.

Review date: 2026-07-18

Prepared by: Research Lead - repository execution agent (Codex)

## Purpose

Determine whether the Stage A evidence base is sufficiently complete and
methodologically adequate to permit formal assessment of C-002 despite E-006,
E-007 and E-008 remaining only provisionally appraised.

This review tests readiness to assess the construct. It does not assess C-002,
change its confidence or lifecycle, claim novelty, design Stage B, create
terminology or canon, or convert missing evidence into negative evidence.

## Decision under review

The available outcomes are:

1. **Gate remains closed** - evidence gaps materially prevent construct
   assessment.
2. **Gate permits a construct assessment while Stage A remains incomplete** -
   the evidence is adequate to assess the construct, subject to explicit
   reservations and reopening conditions.
3. **Gate opens without material reservation** - the evidence is adequate and
   no unresolved gap could materially affect assessment readiness.

**Approved outcome:** **Permit a C-002 construct assessment to proceed while
Stage A remains incomplete.**

This decision does not predetermine whether C-002 should be retained, narrowed,
revised, rejected or deferred. It authorises preparation of a separate,
reviewable construct assessment only after this gate decision is approved. It
does not authorise Stage B work.

## Record considered

The review applies the gate in the
[Stage A protocol](STAGE_A_PROTOCOL.md) and the distinctions in the
[construct and competing-explanation map](CONSTRUCT_MAP.md). It considers:

- independently reviewed records E-004, E-005, E-009 and E-010 through E-015;
- partial Research Lead appraisals and provisional independent reviews for
  E-006 through E-008;
- the component test used throughout appraisal: prior organisational
  availability, later inability to retrieve or apply, and consequential
  reconstruction; and
- the documented absence, in the Stage A record, of a source directly
  establishing all three components.

E-001 through E-003 provide background or internal synthesis but are not used
to cure missing empirical evidence for C-002.

## 1. Coverage adequacy

### Test

Does the reviewed evidence cover the principal neighbouring constructs and
plausible alternative explanations closely enough to assess whether C-002 is
distinguishable without assuming the OME framing?

### Finding

**Adequate for construct assessment, not for claim acceptance.**

| Construct or alternative | Reviewed coverage | Boundary established for assessment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Organisational forgetting and purposeful forgetting | E-004, E-005 | Knowledge loss, consolidation failure, degradation and deliberate forgetting are not equivalent to consequential reconstruction; forgetting can be beneficial. |
| Learning-curve forgetting and performance decay | E-006 partial | Production conditions and omitted variables may explain apparent forgetting; performance decay is not an acceptable direct reconstruction measure. |
| Turnover, knowledge loss and structural buffering | E-007 and E-008 partial | Turnover and delay are proxies or possible mechanisms; structures may buffer effects; neither establishes unavailable reasoning followed by reconstruction. |
| Transactive memory and expertise location | E-009 | Current expertise coordination and team performance are adjacent retrieval mechanisms, not evidence of later loss and reconstruction. |
| Decision and rationale recovery | E-010, E-011 | Recovery can be observed, but the studies do not establish the full temporal sequence or consequential organisational cost. |
| Requirements rework and shared understanding | E-012 | Consequential work can be observed without proof that relevant reasoning was previously available and later inaccessible. |
| Knowledge-transfer stickiness and non-use | E-013 | Knowledge may exist in a source unit yet remain difficult to transfer or apply; this is a strong competing mechanism rather than demonstrated forgetting. |
| Beneficial change, redundancy and independent exploration | E-014, E-015 | Adaptive change and independent solution exploration prevent treating forgetting or parallel work as uniformly harmful; neither study demonstrates beneficial rediscovery. |

The evidence therefore covers the main loss, retrieval, transfer, coordination,
recovery, rework and beneficial-boundary families identified by the Stage A
protocol. It also supplies credible alternatives involving absorptive capacity,
causal ambiguity, coordination, structural buffering, changed context,
adaptive reconfiguration and productive exploration.

The record is adequate to ask whether the three-part working construct is
distinguishable from those neighbours. It is not adequate to infer prevalence,
materiality, causality or novelty.

## 2. Gap materiality

### Test

Could the unverified content in E-006 through E-008 reasonably overturn the
current construct-boundary picture rather than merely refine it?

### Finding

**Material to later confidence and causal interpretation, but not presently
blocking construct assessment.**

| Record | Unverified content | Plausible effect of full appraisal | Gate materiality |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| E-006 | Complete model specification, measures, robustness and limitations for aircraft-production learning and forgetting | Could strengthen, weaken or qualify the omitted-variable warning and the validity of performance decay as a proxy | Unlikely to collapse the distinction between production-cost decay and reconstruction of prior reasoning; material to proxy appraisal, not construct searchability |
| E-007 | Complete operationalisation of turnover, organisational capital, performance and the proposed knowledge-loss mechanism | Could show that knowledge loss is measured more directly than accessible material indicates, or further constrain role and sector contingencies | Could refine a mechanism or boundary, but would overturn the current map only if it directly traced previously available reasoning, later inaccessibility and consequential reconstruction |
| E-008 | Complete construct measures, panel specification, robustness and causal limitations for turnover, structure and project delay | Could alter confidence in structural buffering and the interpretation of organisational-memory proxies | Could refine contingency and mitigation findings, but delay and structural proxies remain conceptually distinct from direct reconstruction unless full text contains an unreported measure of the complete sequence |

The accessible records and provisional reviews already disclose what is not
known. None of the three sources is currently represented as supporting,
conflicting with or directly measuring the complete C-002 chain. Their absence
must constrain any later assessment, but incompleteness alone is not evidence
against C-002 and is not treated as such here.

## 3. Confidence limits

Conditionally opening the gate would not permit conclusions about:

- how often consequential reconstruction occurs;
- its effort, delay, financial cost, error or decision-quality magnitude;
- whether inability to retrieve reasoning causes observed rework or delay;
- whether turnover effects operate through reasoning loss rather than staffing,
  coordination, workload or organisational-capital mechanisms;
- whether structural buffering prevents reconstruction or merely improves
  execution;
- whether performance decay is a defensible proxy for lost reasoning;
- whether beneficial forgetting, unlearning or independent exploration has a
  positive or negative net effect in relevant organisational contexts;
- whether C-002 is empirically supported, false, material or novel; or
- whether the working label is preferable to established vocabulary.

Any formal C-002 construct assessment must report these as unavailable
conclusions. It must not increase confidence because no reviewed source
establishes the complete chain, and it must preserve `Defer` as a legitimate
eventual disposition under the programme's decision rules.

## 4. Reopening conditions

This gate decision must be reopened if later appraisal or new evidence:

- shows that E-006, E-007 or E-008 directly measures all three C-002 components;
- shows that a current proxy was misclassified and is a validated direct measure
  of unavailable reasoning or reconstruction;
- identifies an established construct that subsumes the working construct
  without loss of explanatory content;
- demonstrates that the three components cannot be operationalised without
  circular self-report or retrospective inference;
- attributes apparently consequential reconstruction more adequately to
  ordinary discovery, changed context, staffing, incentives, coordination,
  workload, implementation or adoption failure;
- supplies credible evidence that reconstruction is normally immaterial,
  beneficial or already mitigated by established practices in the relevant
  scope;
- reveals a material source family or disconfirming search family omitted from
  Stage A; or
- changes the lifecycle of a relied-upon record to `Superseded`, `Withdrawn` or
  `Retracted` in a way that removes necessary construct coverage.

Reopening changes the readiness decision; it does not imply that the earlier
decision was improperly made on the evidence then available.

## 5. Permitted next action

If independently approved, this gate review permits one next action:

> Prepare a separate proposed C-002 construct assessment that evaluates whether
> the working construct is sufficiently distinct, scoped and operationalisable
> to justify further investigation.

That assessment must:

- keep E-006 through E-008 visible as incomplete evidence gaps;
- separate direct observations, mechanisms, proxies and competing explanations;
- evaluate the three components independently and together;
- state what would convince the programme it is wrong;
- avoid novelty claims and terminology admission;
- apply the existing retain, narrow, revise, reject or defer rules without
  steering toward retention; and
- receive independent CTO review and accountable approval.

This gate decision does **not** permit Stage B protocol drafting,
additional evidence lifecycle promotion, claim confidence change, canon work,
terminology decisions, governance expansion or field research.

## Gate criteria conclusion

| Existing Stage A gate criterion | Result | Reservation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Distinguishable from ordinary discovery, turnover, non-use, unlearning and performance decay | Sufficient to assess | Distinctions are conceptually and empirically bounded, but the complete construct has not been directly observed in one study. |
| Direct outcomes and acceptable proxies are defined | Sufficient to assess with limits | Recovery and rework are candidate outcomes; turnover, delay and performance decay remain unacceptable as direct measures without an observed link to the three-part sequence. |
| Disconfirming and beneficial alternatives are searchable | Sufficient to assess | Reviewed records identify purposeful forgetting, adaptive change, structural buffering, transfer difficulty and independent exploration; absence of exhaustive search is not saturation. |
| Final search strategy can be frozen without assuming OME terminology | Sufficiently prepared for later review; not frozen here | The construct map supplies constituent vocabulary and non-OME search families, but Stage B design and formal freezing remain outside this review and unauthorised. |

All four criteria are sufficiently developed to permit the narrower construct
assessment, but this review neither freezes a search strategy nor authorises the
protocol's transition into Stage B. The approved gate therefore **permits a
C-002 construct assessment to proceed while Stage A remains incomplete**. The
reservation is material: E-006 through E-008 remain incomplete, no source
establishes the entire sequence, and no inference about validity or novelty is
authorised.

## CTO Independent Gate Review

Status: complete.

- **Coverage adequacy:** adequate. The reviewed families are broad enough to
  evaluate construct boundaries while remaining insufficient for causal,
  prevalence or empirical-support claims.
- **Gap materiality:** non-blocking with explicit reservations. E-006 through
  E-008 remain material to confidence, but their incomplete appraisal does not
  currently prevent readiness for construct assessment.
- **Confidence limits:** appropriate. Distinguishability, empirical support,
  prevalence, causality and novelty remain separate and unavailable conclusions
  are explicit.
- **Reopening conditions:** appropriate and objective, including construct
  subsumption, failure of operationalisability, proxy misclassification,
  omitted evidence families and material evidence-lifecycle changes.
- **Permitted next action:** appropriate. Only preparation of a proposed C-002
  construct assessment is authorised; Stage B, terminology, governance, canon
  and confidence changes remain unauthorised.
- **Independent decision:** approve.
- **Required corrections:** none. A non-blocking wording refinement was
  recommended and incorporated so the conclusion states that a construct
  assessment may proceed while Stage A remains incomplete.

## E-013 - Exploring Internal Stickiness

### Research Lead Source Appraisal

**Full-text access:** complete 17-page publisher article inspected in Wiley's
free-access reader. The publisher DOI is
https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105.

**Registration fidelity:** the citation, sample, analytical method and
contextual direction are accurate. The study analyses 271 questionnaire
observations covering 122 transfers of best practice in eight companies. Its
unit of analysis is a specific transfer from a source unit to a recipient unit,
not an organisation that has forgotten previously shared reasoning.

**Method:** theory development and a retrospective multi-company study tested
with canonical correlation analysis. Respondents assessed transfer episodes
across the initiation, implementation, ramp-up and integration stages. The
study treats internal transfer as replication of an organisational practice,
often containing tacit knowledge and socially embedded routines.

**Finding relevant to C-002:** transfer difficulty was associated more strongly
with knowledge-related and relational factors - especially recipient absorptive
capacity, causal ambiguity and an arduous source-recipient relationship - than
with the conventional motivational explanation. The paper therefore supplies a
credible mechanism by which useful knowledge can exist inside a firm but remain
difficult for another unit to apply.

**C-002 component check:**

| Component | Coverage | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prior organisational availability | Partial, but distributed | A source unit possesses and performs a practice; the study does not show that the recipient previously possessed its reasoning. |
| Later inability to retrieve or apply | Application difficulty observed, temporal loss absent | The recipient encounters transfer stickiness, but this may be first-time transfer rather than later inability to use previously available knowledge. |
| Consequential reconstruction | Not established | Transfer difficulty is assessed; reconstruction of prior reasoning and its organisational cost are not measured. |

**Alternative explanations:** causal ambiguity, low absorptive capacity,
relationship difficulty, implementation complexity and recipient context can
produce poor transfer without forgetting or failed retrieval. A source
practice may also require adaptation rather than faithful reconstruction.

**C-002-relevant limitations:** retrospective perceptual reports; purposively
identified transfer episodes and eight participating companies; canonical
associations rather than an experimental causal design; no transition from
shared availability to later loss; no direct measure of reasoning recovery,
reconstruction work or consequential cost; and a successful source practice is
not necessarily portable to the recipient context.

**Research Lead direction:** `Contextual`. The study is a strong competing
mechanism and construct boundary: knowledge can remain present in one unit yet
be difficult to transfer or apply elsewhere. That is not evidence of the full
C-002 sequence.

## E-014 - Organizational Forgetting, Absorptive Capacity, and Innovation Performance

### Research Lead Source Appraisal

**Full-text access:** complete author-uploaded article inspected. The publisher
DOI is https://doi.org/10.1108/MD-03-2017-0200.

**Registration fidelity:** the citation, 320-company Chinese survey sample and
moderated-mediation analysis are accurate. The registered finding requires an
important qualification: the study reports cross-sectional associations and
does not establish that forgetting causes innovation.

**Method:** seven-point self-report survey answered by a general manager or
marketing head in 320 Chinese high-technology, manufacturing and service
companies. The authors use exploratory factor analysis, hierarchical regression
and bootstrapped conditional indirect effects. The five-item organisational-
forgetting scale asks about introducing knowledge that conflicts with prior
experience, changing product-development and decision processes, changing
information-sharing mechanisms and acquiring technology from different
sources.

**Finding relevant to C-002:** the measured organisational-forgetting construct
was positively associated with innovation performance, partly through
absorptive capacity, and the conditional indirect association was stronger
under greater environmental turbulence. This supplies a counterposition to any
assumption that discarding or replacing prior routines is uniformly harmful.
However, the scale principally captures adaptive reconfiguration and knowledge
acquisition, not demonstrated loss or failed retrieval.

**C-002 component check:**

| Component | Coverage | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prior organisational availability | Not measured as C-002 requires | Prior experience is referenced by scale items, but no specific reasoning record or organisational capability is traced. |
| Later inability to retrieve or apply | Not established | Firms report adaptive change; the study does not measure inability to retrieve or use prior reasoning. |
| Consequential reconstruction | Not established | Innovation performance is self-reported; rediscovery or reconstruction work is not observed. |

**Alternative explanations:** innovative firms may be more willing to report
adaptive change; absorptive capacity may drive both change and innovation;
environmental turbulence, leadership, resources or common-method variance may
explain the associations; and the scale may relabel organisational flexibility
as forgetting.

**C-002-relevant limitations:** cross-sectional, single-respondent self-report
design; no temporal ordering or causal identification; broad cross-industry
sample; construct-validity ambiguity in the forgetting measure; no observation
of knowledge becoming inaccessible; and no measure of rediscovery,
reconstruction or its cost. The authors themselves call for longitudinal and
qualitative research.

**Research Lead direction:** `Contextual`. The source is a useful beneficial-
change counterposition, but it is not evidence of beneficial memory loss and
does not bear directly on the complete C-002 chain.

## E-015 - Facts and Figuring

### Research Lead Source Appraisal

**Full-text access:** complete publisher PDF inspected. The publisher DOI is
https://doi.org/10.1287/orsc.2015.0980.

**Registration fidelity:** the citation, laboratory-network design and
contextual direction are accurate. The population description is tightened:
the experiment runs 16-person networks under four structures rather than
reporting one fixed 16-person sample. The registered interpretation also needs
precision: clustering reduces redundant information search while increasing
convergence in solution search; it does not test rediscovery.

**Method:** laboratory experiment varying four 16-person network structures -
caveman, rewired caveman, hierarchy and ring - in a complex collective
problem-solving task. The design separately measures information exploration
(facts), solution exploration (theories) and performance. Across repeated runs,
the paper reports network-level and individual-position analyses.

**Finding relevant to C-002:** clustered networks coordinated information
search, obtained a more diverse collective fact set and avoided redundant fact
search. The same clustering encouraged participants to copy or converge on
neighbours' theories, reducing diversity in solution space. Clustering improved
collective performance in this task, while the comparable individual-position
effect was not found. The source therefore shows that coordination and
independent exploration have different value in different search spaces.

**C-002 component check:**

| Component | Coverage | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prior organisational availability | Not established | Participants search for task facts and theories; no previously held organisational reasoning is traced. |
| Later inability to retrieve or apply | Not established | Network structure affects exploration and convergence, not later retrieval failure. |
| Consequential reconstruction | Not established | Search redundancy and solution diversity are measured, but rediscovery or reconstruction of lost reasoning is not. |

**Alternative explanations:** task design, incentives, copying, network
position and coordination explain the results without memory loss. Duplicate
fact search may waste effort while independent solution search preserves useful
variation; treating both as one kind of redundancy would erase the paper's main
distinction.

**C-002-relevant limitations:** artificial problem-solving task; short-lived
laboratory networks; repeated-run participation complicates a simple unique-
participant count; performance depends on the task's information and solution
structure; no organisational history, turnover or durable memory; and no loss,
retrieval failure or reconstruction measure.

**Research Lead direction:** `Contextual`. The paper supplies a boundary on
calling all parallel work wasteful. It does not demonstrate beneficial
rediscovery or any component of the C-002 temporal chain.

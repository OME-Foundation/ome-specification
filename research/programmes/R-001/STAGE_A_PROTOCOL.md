# R-001 Stage A protocol

Status: in progress; construct mapping and vocabulary review.

Started: 2026-07-18

Stage A follows the approved [R-001 research programme](../R-001.md). It refines
the working construct, established vocabulary, competing explanations and Stage
B search design. It does not decide C-002.

## Roles

| Role | Current assignment | Independence and limits |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Research Lead | Repository execution agent (Codex) | Selects and registers candidate evidence; cannot solely approve evidence direction or the C-002 decision. |
| Evidence Reviewer | CTO review authority | Independent of repository execution; reviews selection, direction and missing-evidence risks. |
| Architecture Reviewer | CTO review authority | Role overlap with Evidence Reviewer is disclosed; reviews construct boundaries and established terminology. |
| Accountable Approver | Founder | Makes merge decisions; cannot raise evidence confidence by approval. |
| Canon Editor | Unassigned | No canon work is authorised. |
| Implementation Lead | Unassigned | No field experiment or product work is authorised. |

The Evidence Reviewer and Architecture Reviewer are currently the same review
authority. Before any material C-002 disposition or canon consideration, obtain
an additional independent review if that overlap remains.

## Stage A questions

1. Which established constructs overlap the working notion of organisational
   reconstruction?
2. Which constructs describe loss, retrieval failure, non-use, intentional
   removal and beneficial forgetting differently?
3. Which empirical research bears directly on reconstruction effort and which
   only supplies mechanisms or proxies?
4. Which evidence or theory would make C-002 unnecessary, narrower or false?
5. Which vocabulary and populations should enter the Stage B search protocol?

## Initial source families

- organisational forgetting and knowledge depreciation;
- organisational unlearning and intentional forgetting;
- turnover-induced knowledge loss and structural buffering;
- transactive memory systems and expertise location;
- knowledge-transfer stickiness, non-use and adoption failure;
- lost rationale, rediscovery and rework;
- beneficial forgetting, independent verification and productive rediscovery.

## Selection rule

Stage A may include conceptual and review literature for vocabulary mapping.
Only empirical work may directly support or conflict with C-002. Evidence
direction is claim-specific and must state when a measure is merely a proxy for
reconstruction or unavailable reasoning.

Do not exclude a source because it:

- reports a null or beneficial effect;
- uses terminology inconsistent with OME;
- attributes outcomes to a competing explanation; or
- concerns a context where existing practices already mitigate the problem.

## Outputs

- [search log](SEARCH_LOG.md);
- [construct and competing-explanation map](CONSTRUCT_MAP.md);
- registered evidence with independent review pending;
- proposed Stage B vocabulary, eligibility and search amendments; and
- an explicit Stage A gate review.

## Stage A gate

Stage B cannot begin until review determines that:

- the working construct can be distinguished from ordinary discovery, turnover,
  non-use, unlearning and performance decay;
- direct outcomes and acceptable proxies are defined;
- disconfirming and beneficial alternatives are searchable; and
- the final search strategy can be frozen without assuming OME terminology.

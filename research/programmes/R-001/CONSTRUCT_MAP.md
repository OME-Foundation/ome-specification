# R-001 construct and competing-explanation map

Status: Stage A working map; not a finding or OME terminology decision.

## Initial distinctions

| Construct | Established use encountered | Relationship to C-002 | Current caution |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Organisational forgetting | Loss or depreciation of organisational knowledge, routines or capabilities; may be accidental or managed. | Possible antecedent to reconstruction, not reconstruction itself. | The field contains multiple levels and definitions; forgetting can be beneficial. |
| Organisational unlearning | Intentional or facilitated relinquishing of obsolete beliefs or routines. | Competing or boundary construct. | Do not label deliberate adaptation as memory failure. |
| Learning-curve forgetting | Performance/productivity decline after interruption or over time. | Possible proxy for lost retained capability. | Alternative production and omitted-variable explanations can account for apparent forgetting. |
| Turnover-induced knowledge loss | Loss associated with departure of organisational members. | Possible mechanism producing unavailable expertise or context. | Turnover effects are contingent and can be buffered by structures; turnover is not proof of reconstruction. |
| Transactive memory system | Shared knowledge of who knows what and coordinated encoding, storage and retrieval. | Provides an expertise-location and retrieval mechanism. | Team performance associations do not directly measure reconstruction of reasoning. |
| Knowledge-transfer stickiness or non-use | Knowledge exists but transfer, motivation, authority, absorptive capacity or application fails. | Strong competing explanation. | Retrieval may succeed while application fails; do not call every non-use forgetting. |
| Rationale loss | Context, alternatives or reasons behind a decision are unavailable. | Close candidate mechanism for C-002. | Direct empirical consequence evidence has not yet been registered. |
| Rediscovery or rework | Work is repeated or knowledge is developed again. | Candidate observable outcome. | Must distinguish beneficial verification, changed context and ordinary discovery. |

## Early implications for the working construct

The phrase “organisational reconstruction” has not been found as a unified
established construct in the initial search. It remains a neutral working label.
Stage B should search the constituent mechanisms and outcomes rather than the
label alone.

C-002 currently combines at least three propositions:

1. relevant reasoning was previously available to the organisation;
2. it later cannot be retrieved or applied when needed; and
3. actors perform consequential work to reconstruct it.

Evidence for turnover costs, performance decay or weak transactive memory may
bear on one or two propositions without establishing all three.

## What would convince us we are wrong?

- Direct studies find that reconstruction of previously available reasoning is
  rare, immaterial or indistinguishable from ordinary discovery.
- Apparent reconstruction costs are explained more adequately by turnover,
  incentives, workload, coordination, changing context or poor adoption.
- Existing practices reliably preserve or recover reasoning when competently
  implemented, leaving no material integration problem.
- Forgetting or independent rediscovery produces benefits that outweigh the
  alleged costs in the relevant contexts.
- The three parts of C-002 cannot be operationalised or linked without relying
  on circular self-report.

Any of these outcomes would require C-002 to be narrowed, revised, rejected or
deferred. The Stage B protocol must be capable of finding them.

## Unresolved vocabulary searches

- organisational amnesia and knowledge continuity;
- expertise location and transactive retrieval failure;
- knowledge hiding versus unavailable knowledge;
- lost decision rationale and design-rationale recovery;
- requirements rediscovery, rework and intent loss;
- absorptive capacity, internal stickiness and knowledge non-use; and
- beneficial forgetting, unlearning and productive redundancy.

## Stage A source-family findings

Status: provisional observations from registered evidence; independent review
pending.

| Source family | Closest observation | What remains missing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Decision-rationale loss | Architecture-decision recovery directly reconstructs decisions and rationale after original architects become unavailable or forget. | Reported experiments test recovery quality or quantity, not consequential organisational cost or prevalence. |
| Requirements and intent rediscovery | Requirements-related rework is empirically associated with gaps in shared understanding. | The studies do not show that the relevant intent was previously retained and later became inaccessible. |
| Consequential rework | Rework tasks and expensive recovery are observable outcomes in adjacent software studies. | No registered design links measured rework to unavailable prior reasoning across all three C-002 components. |
| Knowledge-transfer stickiness or non-use | Existing practices can remain difficult to transfer because of absorptive capacity, causal ambiguity and source-recipient relationships. | Transfer failure can occur without forgetting or reconstruction and must remain a competing explanation. |
| Beneficial redundancy or rediscovery | Forgetting can correlate with innovation under stated conditions, while independent solution exploration can preserve interpretive diversity. | Neither result demonstrates beneficial reconstruction of lost reasoning; they bound any assumption that forgetting or parallel work is uniformly harmful. |

The searches found no source that directly establishes all three components of
C-002. The closest sources divide the chain: rationale-recovery studies observe
reconstruction without measuring consequence; requirements studies observe
rework without establishing prior availability and retrieval loss; transfer
studies explain non-use without loss; and beneficial-outcome studies challenge
the assumption that forgetting or duplicated exploration is necessarily waste.

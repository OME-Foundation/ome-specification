# Claim Register

Status: Phase 1 research register; not accepted OME canon.

The register contains propositions important enough to affect the justification
or design of OME. Classification and confidence answer different questions:
classification describes the kind of claim; confidence describes the present
strength of its evidence within a stated scope.

## Claim notes

### C-001

**Scope:** the retention-bin model advanced by Walsh and Ungson; later research
may revise its categories without invalidating the broader multi-media claim.

**Alternatives or limitations:** the model has been criticised and extended;
“retained” does not imply current, correct, accessible or beneficial.

**Current assessment:** Moderate rather than High confidence because only one
evidence record has been registered and its conceptual model needs comparison
with later empirical and critical work.

### C-002

**Falsification conditions:** well-designed field evidence shows that inability
to retrieve reasoning rarely causes material reconstruction, delay, error or
decision degradation in the studied contexts, or that a better-supported cause
accounts for the observed effects.

**Current assessment:** initial external evidence is registered but remains
unreviewed and mostly indirect. None yet measures all three required elements:
prior organisational availability, later inability to retrieve or apply the
reasoning, and consequential reconstruction work. The claim must not be used as
an established premise.

### C-003

**Falsification conditions:** existing integrated practices already cover the
proposed lifecycle at sufficient semantic and operational depth, or observed
failures are better explained by adoption and incentives than by a missing
integration.

**Current assessment:** the adjacent-disciplines review identifies partial
coverage but is not evidence that the remaining gap is material in practice.

### C-004

**Falsification conditions:** a specified integrated intervention performs no
better than constituent practices on predefined outcomes, introduces costs or
risks exceeding its benefits, or cannot be implemented with acceptable burden.

**Current assessment:** this is the central intervention hypothesis. No evidence
is registered. OME must not infer efficacy from conceptual coherence.

### C-005

**Scope:** responsible public positioning at the current research stage.

**Current assessment:** the adjacent review demonstrates extensive inheritance
and a plausible integration, while no founding construct has yet demonstrated
novelty or independent empirical validation. Confidence remains Low because the
registered sources are internal and interpretive.

## Lifecycle values

- `Proposed`: recorded but not yet accepted as a research target.
- `Open`: accepted for investigation; assessment incomplete.
- `Assessed`: reviewed under the current evidence and decision rules.
- `Superseded`: replaced by a more precise claim; replacement is recorded.
- `Rejected`: evidence or analysis does not justify retaining the claim.
- `Withdrawn`: removed from active consideration for a documented reason.

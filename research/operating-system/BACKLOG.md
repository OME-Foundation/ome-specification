## Research items

### R-001 — Characterise consequential organisational reconstruction

- **Owner:** Research Lead — repository execution agent (Codex).
- **Started:** 2026-07-18; Stage A construct mapping.
- **Roles and independence:** recorded in the [Stage A protocol](../programmes/R-001/STAGE_A_PROTOCOL.md).
- **Programme design:** [R-001 research programme](../programmes/R-001.md).
- **Question:** when, how and with what consequences do organisations
  reconstruct reasoning that was previously available but cannot be retrieved
  or applied?
- **Why it matters:** C-002 is part of the problem statement but currently lacks
  registered empirical support.
- **Scope:** begin with knowledge-intensive delivery and change work; distinguish
  forgetting, inaccessible knowledge, invalid knowledge, staff turnover,
  deliberate rediscovery and ordinary discovery of something never known.
- **Method:** staged construct mapping and focused evidence review followed by
  an explicit gate on whether field research is justified. Search for
  counterexamples and alternative causal explanations.
- **Outputs:** construct definition, mechanism map, evidence records, boundary
  conditions and revised assessment of C-002.
- **Completion evidence:** registered sources cover supporting, conflicting and
  inconclusive findings; terms are mapped to established disciplines; remaining
  empirical gaps are explicit.
- **Non-goals:** estimating a universal cost of forgetting; collecting customer
  evidence in the public repository; inventing an OME metric.

### R-002 — Test the claimed cross-lifecycle coverage gap

- **Owner:** Unassigned.
- **Started:** Not started.
- **Question:** do existing disciplines and integrated practices inadequately
  connect objectives, evidence, decisions, delivery artifacts, outcomes and
  learning, or is the perceived gap primarily adoption failure?
- **Why it matters:** a conceptual gap cannot justify a new engineering approach
  if established methods already solve the operational problem.
- **Scope:** include named practices and tools that integrate KM, traceability,
  decision rationale, provenance, EA and learning—not only each field in
  isolation.
- **Method:** focused systematic search with inclusion/exclusion criteria,
  forward/backward citation searching, practice-framework comparison and active
  search for disconfirming cases.
- **Outputs:** coverage matrix, closest integrated alternatives, evidence
  records, remaining gap statement or rejection of C-003.
- **Completion evidence:** at least one serious alternative explanation and the
  strongest existing integrated approaches are evaluated on equal terms.
- **Non-goals:** proving novelty from vocabulary; treating lack of discovery as
  proof of nonexistence.

### R-003 — Specify the minimal integrated intervention

- **Owner:** Unassigned.
- **Started:** Not started.
- **Question:** what is the smallest practice that represents the alleged OME
  integration without rebuilding established tools or imposing excessive
  capture cost?
- **Dependencies:** R-001 and R-002 must identify a material, bounded gap.
- **Method:** intervention modelling and adversarial design review.
- **Outputs:** actors, information objects, relationships, workflow, authority,
  costs, failure modes and falsifiable mechanisms.
- **Completion evidence:** every component maps to an established source or a
  separately classified proposal; removal tests show why each component exists.
- **Non-goals:** product architecture, API design, screens or implementation
  backlog.

### R-004 — Define comparative outcomes and study design

- **Owner:** Unassigned.
- **Started:** Not started.
- **Question:** how would an integrated intervention be compared with ordinary
  ADR, traceability and knowledge-management practice?
- **Dependencies:** a testable intervention from R-003.
- **Method:** preregistration-oriented study design with construct-validity and
  burden analysis.
- **Outputs:** separately reported outcomes for retrieval, explanation,
  challenge, validity, trace coverage, adaptation, cost and unintended effects;
  sampling and analysis plan.
- **Completion evidence:** outcomes have operational definitions, plausible
  baselines, confounders, failure thresholds and anti-gaming analysis.
- **Non-goals:** a scalar Organisational Memory Index or a universal maturity
  score.

## Status values

- `Proposed`: not yet accepted into the active research programme.
- `Ready`: question, scope, method and decision outcome are sufficiently clear.
- `In progress`: active work has an identified owner and recorded start date.
- `Blocked`: a named dependency prevents responsible progress.
- `In review`: outputs and evidence records are ready for critical review.
- `Complete`: the stated research decision was made and outputs are linked.
- `Cancelled`: stopped with a documented reason; motivating claims remain
  separately assessed.

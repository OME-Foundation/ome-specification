# Confidence and decision rules

Status: proposed research governance; not accepted OME canon.

## Confidence is a judgement, not a score

OME does not calculate confidence by counting citations or averaging arbitrary
weights. Confidence is a structured judgement about a precisely scoped claim.
The assessment must explain:

- directness: does the evidence test the claim or merely provide context?
- methodological fitness: can the method answer the question posed?
- relevance: do population, domain, setting and outcome match the claim?
- independence: do sources provide independent information or repeat one origin?
- consistency: what explains agreement and disagreement across evidence?
- precision: are the construct, effect and boundaries sufficiently specified?
- bias and alternatives: what selection, publication, measurement or reasoning
  biases and alternative explanations remain?
- reproducibility: can another reviewer inspect or reproduce the basis?

## Confidence values

| Value | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Unassessed | Evidence has not yet received a claim-level assessment. This is not “low.” |
| Very low | Evidence is indirect, sparse or seriously limited; the claim should not guide consequential conclusions. |
| Low | Some relevant evidence exists, but important limitations or alternatives remain unresolved. |
| Moderate | Multiple relevant lines of evidence support a bounded claim; meaningful uncertainty or counter-evidence remains. |
| High | Strong, relevant and substantially independent evidence supports the bounded claim, with alternatives and counter-evidence adequately addressed. |

`High` is not certainty. Confidence always applies to the recorded wording and
scope, not to a memorable label.

## Claim decisions

After review, make one explicit disposition:

- **Retain:** current wording, classification and scope remain justified.
- **Narrow:** retain only a more limited population, mechanism, outcome or
  context.
- **Revise:** change the proposition or classification; retain the decision
  history.
- **Reject:** evidence or analysis does not justify the claim.
- **Supersede:** replace it with one or more more precise claims and link them.
- **Defer:** available evidence cannot discriminate; state what would resolve it.

The reviewer records the evidence considered, counter-evidence, alternatives,
scope, confidence, decision and date. Workflow completion alone never produces
a `Retain` decision.

## Decision thresholds

### Public research conclusion

A conclusion may be stated publicly when:

- the claim and scope are precise;
- its adjacent-disciplinary relationship is documented;
- material supporting, conflicting and inconclusive evidence is represented;
- the evidence provenance and limitations are reviewable;
- confidence is assessed rather than implied;
- alternative explanations receive a fair treatment; and
- the wording does not exceed the evidence.

Low-confidence findings may be published as low-confidence research when their
uncertainty is the point. They must not be promoted as settled conclusions.

### Candidate for canon consideration

Research may be considered for canon only when all public-research requirements
pass and:

- dependent empirical claims are normally at least Moderate confidence;
- established terminology is used unless a defended distinction survives
  review;
- the proposition is stable enough to guide practice;
- consequences, failure modes and boundary conditions are documented; and
- a substantive pull request receives explicit review and founder approval.

This threshold permits consideration; it does not require canonisation.
Principles require evidence-informed justification and trade-off analysis rather
than pretending a normative choice has empirical confidence.

### Rejection or removal

A claim should be rejected, narrowed or superseded when:

- its terms cannot be operationalised sufficiently for its intended use;
- stronger evidence supports an incompatible explanation;
- the claimed distinction from established work disappears;
- the integration adds no demonstrated explanatory or practical value; or
- foreseeable cost, harm or gaming makes the proposed use irresponsible.

Negative findings are progress. Records remain addressable so that rejected
ideas are not silently rediscovered.

## Classification changes

Confidence and classification are independent. Strong evidence may establish a
hypothesis within OME's studies without making it `Established` across a field.
Moving to `Established` requires a credible external body of knowledge using
substantially the same concept. Moving to `Synthesis` requires a defined and
useful integration of named constituents. A new label alone changes neither.

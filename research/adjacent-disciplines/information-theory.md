# Information theory and communication loss

## Established definition and frameworks

### Source findings

Shannon's mathematical theory measures uncertainty and the capacity of a
communication channel to transmit symbols in the presence of noise. It
deliberately abstracts away from meaning. Shannon entropy is therefore not a
ready-made measure of lost intent, rationale, or shared understanding.

Human communication research supplies closer concepts. Clark and Brennan's
grounding theory treats communication as collaborative work to establish
sufficient mutual understanding. Carlile describes syntactic, semantic, and
pragmatic knowledge boundaries, with progressively demanding processes of
transfer, translation, and transformation. Szulanski's “internal stickiness”
identifies causal ambiguity, lack of absorptive capacity, and difficult source-
recipient relationships as barriers to knowledge transfer.

### OME interpretation

The phrase **Translation Loss** is not a recognised unified metric for the
founding OME problem, but the underlying phenomenon is not novel. It overlaps
semantic/pragmatic knowledge-boundary failures, grounding failures,
requirements communication gaps, knowledge-transfer stickiness, information
loss, and intent drift.

OME should not use Shannon entropy as scientific decoration. A defensible model
would specify what is transformed, the reference state, the receiver's required
understanding, observable discrepancies, and the cost or consequence of those
discrepancies.

## Problems handled well

- Information theory: signal uncertainty, coding, noise, and channel capacity.
- Grounding: mutual understanding in situated communication.
- Knowledge-boundary theory: transfer, translation, and transformation across
  syntactic, semantic, and pragmatic boundaries.
- Knowledge-transfer research: barriers between source and recipient.

## Limitations relevant to OME

No reviewed framework alone measures the full degradation of business intent
across multi-stage organisational delivery. However, OME must demonstrate that
combining them yields predictive or actionable value.

## OME overlap and decision

| Founding idea | Assessment |
| --- | --- |
| Meaning degrades across handoffs | **Established**. |
| Translation Loss as an umbrella label | **Synthesis**; retain provisionally with explicit antecedents. |
| Translation Loss Index | **Hypothesis** and currently under-specified. |
| Loss is additive across handoffs | **Open question**; compounding, correction, and transformation can violate additivity. |
| More documentation necessarily reduces loss | **Discard**; unsupported. |

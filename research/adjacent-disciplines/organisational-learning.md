# Organisational learning

## Established definition and frameworks

### Source findings

Organisational learning studies how experience changes knowledge, behaviour,
routines, and organisational codes. Argyris and Schön distinguished
single-loop learning (correcting action within governing assumptions) from
double-loop learning (questioning those assumptions). March modelled the
tension between exploitation of known capabilities and exploration of new
possibilities, showing why adaptive systems can become efficient in the short
term yet fragile in the long term.

Recognised terminology includes single-loop and double-loop learning,
exploration, exploitation, organisational routines, feedback, feed-forward,
absorptive capacity, and learning-by-doing.

### OME interpretation

OME should not equate memory with learning. Memory concerns retained and
retrievable experience; learning requires change in understanding or action.
Capturing a retrospective without changing future behaviour is retention, not
completed learning.

The founding phrase “learning completes the cycle” is best retained as a
**Principle**, not presented as a discovered law. An OME implementation should
link outcomes back to assumptions and decisions and should expose whether
lessons altered rules, models, or work.

## Problems handled well

- Explaining adaptation from experience and feedback.
- Distinguishing incremental correction from reframing assumptions.
- Explaining path dependence and the exploration/exploitation trade-off.
- Showing why successful routines can inhibit future learning.

## Limitations relevant to OME

The literature does not give a single operational data model for connecting a
specific objective, decision, implementation, outcome, and later revision.
Learning is also difficult to infer from document production alone.

## OME overlap and possible gap

| Founding idea | Assessment |
| --- | --- |
| Outcomes must update organisational memory | **Principle**. |
| Decision histories enable learning | **Hypothesis**; histories enable but do not cause learning. |
| A closed objective-to-learning chain | **Synthesis** of feedback learning and traceability. |
| Learning can be scored from artefact completeness | **Open question** and likely invalid without behavioural evidence. |

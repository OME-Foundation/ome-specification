# Knowledge management

## Established definition and frameworks

### Source findings

Knowledge management (KM) already addresses the systematic creation,
acquisition, sharing, application, retention, and improvement of organisational
knowledge. Nonaka's knowledge-creation theory distinguishes tacit and explicit
knowledge and describes socialisation, externalisation, combination, and
internalisation. ISO 30401 specifies requirements and guidance for establishing,
implementing, maintaining, reviewing, and improving a knowledge-management
system.

Established vocabulary includes tacit knowledge, explicit knowledge, knowledge
creation, knowledge sharing, knowledge transfer, communities of practice,
lessons learned, intellectual capital, knowledge retention, and knowledge
management systems.

### OME interpretation

OME must not claim to invent managed organisational knowledge, knowledge
lifecycles, or the idea that knowledge is a governed asset. It should use KM
terminology unless a narrower construct is required.

OME's credible distinction could be an engineering specialisation focused on
the traceable transformation of intent and reasoning through complex delivery,
with explicit evidence, temporal change, and outcome validation. That is a
scope claim, not proof of a new discipline.

## Problems handled well

- Knowledge capture, sharing, retention, and reuse.
- Governance and continual improvement of KM systems.
- Tacit/explicit knowledge interaction.
- Organisational practices and incentives around knowledge.

## Limitations relevant to OME

KM programmes often lack artifact-level, bidirectional traceability from
business intent to implementation. KM guidance can also remain management-level
and under-specify evidence, contradiction, supersession, and machine-readable
relationships. These are tendencies, not universal defects.

## OME overlap and possible gap

| Founding idea | Assessment |
| --- | --- |
| Organisational knowledge lifecycle | **Established** at the broad level. |
| Human-owned organisational truth | **Principle**. |
| Evidence-linked, versioned reasoning graph | **Synthesis** of KM, provenance, traceability, and semantic modelling. |
| OME as a replacement for KM | **Discard**; unjustified. |
